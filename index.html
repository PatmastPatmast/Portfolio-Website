My Work

A selection of my range of work

Clothing as a carrier of meaning. PATMAST translates cultural concepts (e.g. Taiwanese mythology, Japanese aesthetics, Chinese visual traditions) into contemporary visual forms.

Visual Research: Taiwanese Women’s Fashion (1900–1945)

A visual study on the transformation of female silhouettes in Taiwan under colonial, cultural, and modern influences.

  • Silhouette as cultural language
  • East–West hybridization
  • Clothing as social and ideological marker

Guardian of the Modern City

This project reimagines the Taiwanese city god Cheng Huang as a figure that exists in a contemporary urban environment, using fashion and character design to connect mythology with everyday city life.

  • Translating a traditional deity into a modern visual language
  • Using clothing to express responsibility, authority, and place
  • Exploring how mythology can feel present in contemporary cities
